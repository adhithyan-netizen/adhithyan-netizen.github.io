As a Mechanical Engineering student at Saintgits College of Engineering, I am deeply passionate about pushing the boundaries of technology in automotive systems, aerospace, and thermodynamics. My engineering journey is driven by a relentless curiosity to design and optimize complex mechanical systems. Currently, I apply my theoretical knowledge to high-performance scenarios as a member of the Division of Braking for Madbolt Formula Racing, where I utilize tools like SolidWorks, ANSYS, and CFD to engineer precision components. I am also actively engaged with professional communities like SAEINDIA, IEI, and ISNT to stay at the forefront of industry advancements. My hands-on experience spans across multiple research and system development roles, notably as a System Development Engineer at Tessat. I have conceptualized and developed a diverse portfolio of advanced projects, ranging from thermal management systems for EV batteries and landslide rescue rovers to vacuum-integrated kinetic energy recovery systems. These endeavors have honed my skills in CAD, fluid dynamics, and machine design, allowing me to transform abstract concepts into tangible, functional prototypes that address pressing mechanical challenges. Innovation and intellectual property are at the core of my professional identity. I have successfully filed twelve patents spanning autonomous navigation, advanced energy storage, and safety apparatuses. Beyond technical research, I actively cultivate my leadership and organizational skills through roles such as Technical Team Member for TEDx Saintgits and Marketing Manager for Nk'26. I am eager to connect with fellow innovators and industry professionals as I continue to build solutions that drive engineering excellence forward.
